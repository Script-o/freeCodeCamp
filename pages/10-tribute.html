Desmond Tutu

Archbishop Emeritus of Cape Town and Nobel Peace Laureate

Who Was Desmond Tutu?

He was a man who was committed to non-violence and stood in opposition to the apartheid in South Africa for over 40 years. He fought for peace, reconciliation, and healing for the people of Africa. He spread kindness and joy in his many writings, teaching others how to forgive even in the harshest of human evils.

Here is a timeline of Archbishop Desmond Tutu's life

  • 1931 Oct. 7 - Desmond Mpilo Tutu is born in Klerksdorp, near Johannesburg.
  • 1947 Tutu suffers from tuberculosis while at Sophiatown, Johannesburg.
  • 1955 Marries Nomalizo Leah Shenxane and teaches at a high school in Johannesburg.
  • 1958 Resigns from teaching and begins his ordination training at St Peters Theological College, in Johannesburg.
  • 1961 Is ordained as a minister in the Anglican church.
  • 1962 Moves to Britain to study theology at King's College London.
  • 1967 Returns to the Eastern Cape of Alice, South Africa to teach at the Federal Theological Seminary.
  • 1975 Becomes the Anglican Church's first Black dean of Johannesburg.
  • 1978 Becomes first black general-secretary of the South African Council of Churches. Achieves global prominence as a leading opponent of apartheid, supports economic sanctions to achieve majority rule in South Africa.
  • 1980 As general secretary of the South African Council of Churches, Tutu leads a delegation of church leaders to meet Prime Minister PW Botha, urging him to end apartheid. Although nothing comes of the meeting, it is a historical moment where a Black leader confronts a senior white government official. The government confiscates Tutu's passport.
  • 1984 16 October, Desmond Tutu is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in recognition of his endeavours for a non-violent end to apartheid.
  • 1985 Tutu becomes the first Black bishop of Johannesburg. He publicly endorses an economic boycott of South Africa and civil disobedience as a way to dismantle apartheid.
  • 1986 Tutu becomes the first Black person appointed bishop of Cape Town and head of the Anglican Church of the Province of Southern Africa. With other church leaders, he mediates conflicts between Black protesters and government security forces.
  • 1989 Leads anti-apartheid march of 30,000 people through Cape Town.
  • 1990 Hosts Nelson Mandela for his first night of freedom after Mandela is released from prison after being held for 27 years for his opposition to apartheid. Mandela calls Tutu "the people's archbishop."
  • 1995 Mandela asks Tutu to chair the Truth and Reconciliation Commission that was being set up to "deal with what happened under apartheid" by investigating human rights violations, offering support to victims, and scrutinising applications for amnesty from perpetrators of past violations.
  • 1996 Tutu retires from the church to focus solely on the commission. He continues his activism, advocating for equality and reconciliation and is later named Archbishop Emeritus of Cape Town.
  • 1997 Tutu is diagnosed with prostate cancer. He has been hospitalised to treat recurring infections.
  • 2005 Presented with the International Gandhi Peace Prize
  • 2009 Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom from U.S. President Barack Obama.
  • 2012 Desmond Tutu is awarded $1 million dollars by the Mo Ibrahim Foundation for 'his lifelong commitment to speaking the truth to power'.
  • 2013 Dubbed "the moral compass of the nation", Tutu declares his support for gay rights, saying he would never "worship a God who is homophobic".
  • 2016 Archbishop Emeritus Desmond Tutu and his wife Leah received the Award for Peace with Justice for their contribution to social justice.
  • 2021 Dec. 26 - Tutu dies in Cape Town, aged 90.
